Benovia: Cozy Fireside Wine Tasting

December 13, 2016
sponsored by:
Stacy Briscoe
  • share

Cozy Fireside Wine Tasting

Driving through the entrance gates and down a vineyard-lined gravel path, you can't help but think "I've arrived" -- arrived in Sonoma County, arrived in California wine country. Even the short walk from the parking lot to the cabin-like tasting room will take you past rows of vines so close you can reach out and touch them from the footpath.

Once inside, you'll see that Benovia is, indeed, just as cozy as a cabin, complete with a roaring fire. The by-appointment tastings take place in a living room with guests and host seated casually and comfortably on over-stuffed couches with the wine flights displayed on the center coffee table. And because the winery only conducts one group at a time, you're sure to receive one-on-one attention from an employee who works directly with the winemaker, thus both knowledgeable and happy enough to answer any questions you may have.

As Benovia is a small lot winery, producing just about 6000 cases a year, the tasting will be limited to current releases. For $20 per guest, the host will provide a full line up of both red and white wines, which, on a recent visit, included single vineyard Chardonnays and Pinot Noirs from both Benovia estate vineyards as well as long-standing growing partner, Martinelli Vineyards. What you can expect from Benovia winemaker Mike Sullivan is a diverse portfolio of California wines that truly speak of each specific vineyard from which the grapes were harvested. Like the 2013 La Pommeraie Chardonnay, with its funky, floral aroma and its almost piquant palate of fruit and baking spices, it seems to pay homage to the once thriving apple orchard for which the wine is named; or the 2014 Tilton Hill Pinot Noir, with its bold rusticity and almost lack of structure, you can visualize, as you taste, the small, purposefully dense vineyard with grapes and vines overlapping as they fight for the Sonoma Coast sun.
